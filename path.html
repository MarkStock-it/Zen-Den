Therapy isn't one-size-fits-all. We draw from several evidence-based approaches, choosing what fits you, not the other way around.

Cognitive Behavioural Therapy

Changing how you think

CBT is one of the most widely studied forms of therapy in the world. It's based on a powerful insight: the way we think shapes how we feel, and how we feel shapes what we do.

In sessions, we gently identify thought patterns that may be keeping you stuck, all-or-nothing thinking, catastrophising, or self-blame, then work to reshape them into more balanced, compassionate perspectives.

CBT is particularly effective for anxiety, depression, OCD, phobias, and stress. Sessions are structured and collaborative, you are always an equal partner.

🧠

Identify patterns

Recognise the automatic thoughts that trigger distress, often without you even noticing.

🔍

Challenge gently

Examine the evidence for and against those thoughts and not to dismiss them, but to see clearly.

🌿

Build new habits

Practice different responses until they feel natural, a new relationship with your own mind.

Dialectical Behaviour Therapy

Holding two truths at once

DBT was developed for people who experience emotions very intensely, but its tools are useful for almost anyone. The core idea is the "dialectic": two things can be true at once. You can accept yourself exactly as you are, and still want to grow.

DBT teaches four key skill sets: mindfulness (staying present), distress tolerance (surviving difficult moments), emotion regulation (understanding your feelings), and interpersonal effectiveness (communicating your needs clearly).

🌊
Mindfulness

Presence

Observing your thoughts and feelings without judgement, the skill that underpins all others.

🛟

Resilience

Riding out crisis moments without reacting in ways that create new problems.

💬
Interpersonal

Connection

Asking for what you need clearly and kindly, while respecting yourself and others.

☁️
Emotion Regulation

Balance

Understanding the cycle of emotions and reducing vulnerability to intense emotional swings.

Mindfulness-Based Therapy

Returning to the present

Much of our suffering comes from living in the past (rumination) or the future (anxiety). Mindfulness-based approaches teach you to gently anchor your attention to the here and now.

You don't need to meditate for hours. Even small, intentional pauses throughout your day can profoundly shift your relationship with stress, low mood, and the constant pull of a busy mind.

Not sure which approach is right for you? That's completely normal, it's exactly what a first session is for.
